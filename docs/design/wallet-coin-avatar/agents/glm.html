CCX · wallet avatar · round 2 · GLM

Three materials, none of them a coin.

Round 1 collapsed onto a dark metal disc. This round picks three genuinely different visual languages — a flat brutalist tile, a warm frosted-glass chip, and a pressed-paper seal — so the wallet avatar can be told apart by material, not just by letter. Every variant below is drawn in pure HTML+CSS (system font, no images, no JS), shown at 24 / 28 / 32 / 36 px with initials M · S · A, on both a dark #1a1714 and a light #f3efe9 swatch.

Ember Tile

brutalist monogram

A flat square of warm charcoal, an orange letter carved in, and a single faceted orange corner — a quiet nod to the polygon geometry of the Conceal mark. No gradients, no metal: it's pure typography plus one geometric accent, which is exactly why it stays crisp at 24px. On the light swatch it inverts to a white block with a hard charcoal offset — same poster energy, opposite polarity.

Glassine Chip

warm frosted glass

A soft squircle of warm smoked glass — not the generic blue glassy blob, but a translucent amber-tinted chip with a thin orange hairline glowing down the left edge as the brand signature. The letter sits weight-600 in warm white on dark, deep charcoal on light, with a 1px inner-top highlight carrying the "glass" read. At 24px the silhouette is just a clean rounded square with an orange thread — legible — and the material richness opens up as it scales up.

Letterpress Seal

pressed paper

A deckle-edged cream sticker with a debossed monogram and a single orange rule beneath the letter — a bookplate, a waxless seal, a piece of paper. It's the warmest of the three: opaque, soft, tactile, intentionally a little imperfect (the organic radius). On dark it pops as a bright tag; on light it layers as white-on-cream with a whisper-soft shadow. The orange rule is the only brand cue — and it's enough.

Why three materials

Matte print, warm glass, pressed paper — different silhouettes, different surface logic. Whichever direction wins, the wallet system gets a look no other crypto app is wearing right now.

Legibility at 24px

Letters run 0.58–0.6em of the avatar with weight 600–800, so the glyph owns the tile. No stroke thinner than ~1.5px appears at 24, and every accent is geometric (wedge, hairline, rule) so nothing dithers into mud.

Brand thread

Orange lives in exactly one place per variant — the corner wedge, the left hairline, the under-rule — so the brand reads instantly without dyeing the whole avatar. Easy to tint per-wallet later by recoloring that single accent.
